History of Humanoid Robots

Humanoid robots have evolved tremendously since their inception. From simple mechanical structures to sophisticated machines that can mimic human actions, the journey of humanoid robots has been a fascinating one.

  • 1921 - The First Concept of a Humanoid Robot

    The term "robot" was first introduced by playwright Karel Čapek in his play R.U.R. (Rossum’s Universal Robots).

  • 1973 - WABOT-1

    WABOT-1, the first full-scale humanoid robot, was created in Japan and could walk and grasp objects.

  • 2000 - ASIMO by Honda

    Honda introduced ASIMO, a highly advanced humanoid robot capable of running, climbing stairs, and interacting with humans.

  • 2020 - Advanced AI Integration

    With advancements in AI, humanoid robots like Sophia are now capable of human-like conversations and expressions.
